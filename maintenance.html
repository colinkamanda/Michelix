Hardware Maintenance

When looking to improve your computer's perfomance, a commonly overlooked factor is computer Hardware maintenance. Most people purchase new parts or even buy a brand-new device whenever they encounter computer errors and faulty computer Hardware components. There are basic actions you can take to improve your computer's perfomance immediately.
PC hardware maintenance is the process of auditing, upgrading, and maintening a computer's physical parts to ensure the components perform optimally. IT hardware maintenance involves caring for components such as the Keyboard, CD drives, hard disk, batttery, and other peripherals.

PC hardware maintenance primarily involves cleaning, repairing, testing, and upgrading the physical components of your computer. The components that requires inspection during IT hardware maintenance procedures are:

. CPU fan heatsing - ensure the fan spins freely witout hiccups and rotates when you switch the power on.

. RAM components -check that RAM memory modules are seated securely on the motherboard and retaining clips are in place (if fitted).

. Storage components -ensure that both internal and extenal devices connect properly to the computer and are discoverable. For examples, sometimes, when you cannot discover a hard drive on your computer's setting, it can be due to loose cables.

. screws -ensure all parts are screwed in firmly witn no loose nuts and bolts.

. Power supply - Inspect the AC power unit and ensure your computer's cord sits firmly on the output socket. Also, examine the DC power connectors and see taht they connect as expected.

. Keyboard and mouse - use compressed air dusters to blast off debris from your Keyboard and mouse. Ensure the mouse works consistenly and make replacements where necessary.

. Expansion card - ensure your computer's expansion card (also known as adapter card) is placed firmly and securely with the retainers screws.

. Cabling -analyze-all cables to check for proper connectivity. Utilize ties to gather and route lines to prevent accidents. Also, label your cables for easier identification, especially if multiple devices connect to one port.
